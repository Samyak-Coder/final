Apple TVs

Apple TV has 2 generations the first with HD resolution and the second with 4K resolution.

First Generation

The Apple TV is a digital media player with a new Operating System named tvOS with an App Store, allowing downloads of third-party apps for video, audio, games and other content.Upon release, third-party apps were available from a limited range of providers, with new APIs providing opportunities for more apps. A requirement of new apps and games was that they must include interfacing with the new touchpad-enabled Siri remote, which was later relaxed for games.

Second Generation

The Apple TV 4K is a digital media player and microconsole. The first-generation model introduced support for 2160p output, HDR10, Dolby Vision and includes an Apple A10X Fusion processor supporting HEVC hardware decoding.This model was also introduced with SIRI remote with IR support with Bluetooth 5.0
